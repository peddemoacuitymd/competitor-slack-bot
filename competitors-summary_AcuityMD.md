# Competitive Analysis: AcuityMD

**Source**: https://www.acuitymd.com
**Date**: 2026-02-19
**Category**: MedTech commercial intelligence / medical device sales enablement

## Target Company Summary

AcuityMD is a MedTech intelligence platform that combines healthcare data, AI-powered insights, and sales workflows purpose-built for medical device commercialization. It targets commercial teams at device companies — from pre-revenue startups to global enterprises — and positions on speed ("3x faster sales process"), data precision, and a UI "reps actually use." Trusted by 400+ medical device companies. Pricing is not public; requires a demo.

## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### Veeva Systems (MedTech Cloud)
**URL**: https://www.veeva.com/medtech/
**Positioning**: "Unifying the Total MedTech Product Lifecycle." Enterprise cloud platform connecting clinical, quality, regulatory, and commercial functions in one system. Customers include Medtronic, BD, Philips, GE Healthcare.
**Pricing**: Enterprise contracts only. Estimated ~$142/user/mo for large deployments; higher per-seat for smaller teams. AI features free through 2030.
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Full lifecycle platform (clinical trials through post-market surveillance) — AcuityMD covers commercial only
- Massive installed base with 1,100+ life sciences customers and deep enterprise trust
- CRM, content management, events, email, and compliance in one integrated suite
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Built for large enterprises — heavyweight implementation, long sales cycles, high cost; poor fit for startups
- CRM is generic life sciences, not purpose-built for medtech's procedure-driven sales motions
- Transitioning from legacy Salesforce CRM to Vault CRM — customers face migration complexity

### Definitive Healthcare
**URL**: https://www.definitivehc.com
**Positioning**: "The definitive picture of healthcare." Broad healthcare data and analytics covering hospitals, physicians, payers, and claims. 9,000+ hospital profiles, 3M+ healthcare professionals, billions of claims records.
**Pricing**: Not public. Custom quotes with a la carte, bundle, and add-on options. Scales from startup to enterprise.
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Broadest healthcare data coverage — hospitals, IDNs, payers, physicians, consumers in one platform
- Serves multiple industries beyond medtech (pharma, health IT, consulting, finance) — bigger data moat
- De-identified consumer data (225M patients) enables market sizing and demand modeling AcuityMD lacks (inferred)
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Horizontal healthcare platform, not medtech-specific — no understanding of device sales workflows, territories, or procedure-level targeting
- No built-in CRM or sales workflow tools — data must be exported into separate systems
- Overwhelming for medtech reps who need actionable targets, not raw data exploration

### Alpha Sophia
**URL**: https://www.alphasophia.com
**Positioning**: "Take the guesswork out of healthcare targeting." Affordable, transparent commercial intelligence for MedTech startups and SMBs. Emphasizes instant access, intuitive UI, and cost-effectiveness vs. incumbents.
**Pricing**: Custom quote. Max plan includes 5 users with access to ~80% of US medical claims (300M+ patient lives). Custom plan adds API access and consulting.
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Positions explicitly as cheaper and faster to deploy — no lengthy onboarding, instant platform access
- Built-in CRM with export capabilities — operationalize leads without a separate tool
- KOL AI feature for publication and citation analysis — useful for clinical marketing that AcuityMD doesn't highlight
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Smaller company with less brand recognition and fewer enterprise logos
- Claims ~80% of US medical claims vs. AcuityMD's positioning as comprehensive — potential data coverage gap
- Less sophisticated sales workflow tooling — better for targeting than full pipeline management

### IQVIA MedTech
**URL**: https://www.iqvia.com (MedTech division)
**Positioning**: "Global partner for accelerating MedTech innovation." Combines the world's largest healthcare dataset with OCE (Orchestrated Customer Engagement) CRM, real-world evidence, and consulting services. Full lifecycle from clinical development through commercial.
**Pricing**: Enterprise contracts only. Not public. Known to be premium-priced.
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Largest healthcare dataset in the world — unmatched claims, Rx, and real-world data depth
- OCE CRM platform with embedded real-time intelligence and multichannel engagement
- Global footprint with consulting, analytics, and technology under one umbrella
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Massive, complex organization — slow sales cycles, heavy implementation, requires dedicated admin
- OCE is Salesforce-based — carries platform complexity and cost overhead
- Primarily pharma-oriented; medtech is a secondary vertical, not core DNA

### Carevoyance (by Definitive Healthcare)
**URL**: https://www.definitivehc.com (acquired asset)
**Positioning**: Procedure-level commercial intelligence purpose-built for medical device sales and marketing. Acquired by Definitive Healthcare. Focuses on targeting physicians by procedure volume, facility analysis, and territory planning.
**Pricing**: Custom quote (bundled with Definitive Healthcare offerings post-acquisition).
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Deep procedure-level analytics — CPT code volume by physician and facility, directly comparable to AcuityMD's core offering
- Backed by Definitive Healthcare's broader data assets post-acquisition — can layer hospital, IDN, and payer data
- Established brand among medtech sales teams pre-acquisition
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Post-acquisition integration with Definitive Healthcare has diluted the standalone product identity (inferred)
- Less modern UI/UX than AcuityMD — AcuityMD emphasizes "a format reps actually use"
- No AI-powered insights layer comparable to AcuityMD's positioning around intelligent recommendations

### MedScout
**URL**: https://www.medscout.io
**Positioning**: "Actionable Intelligence for Medical Sales." Revenue acceleration platform translating 34B+ claims (310M+ patients) into actionable insights for medtech sales teams. Founded 2020, Austin TX. $20.8M total funding (Seed led by Stage 2 Capital, with LiveOak, Alumni Ventures, a16z Scout).
**Pricing**: Custom quote. Not public.
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Very similar value proposition — claims-based physician targeting purpose-built for medtech reps, with emphasis on territory intelligence and actionable insights
- Strong early traction with reported customer results (40% growth claims)
- Well-funded with $20.8M raised; backed by credible investors (a16z Scout, Stage 2 Capital)
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Smaller, earlier-stage company with less brand recognition and fewer enterprise logos
- Narrower platform scope — focused on sales targeting, less visible workflow/pipeline tooling
- Less established customer base compared to AcuityMD's 400+ medical device companies

### RepSignal (S2N Health)
**URL**: https://s2nhealth.com/repsignal/
**Positioning**: "Sales Intelligence for Med Device Companies." AI-powered commercial intelligence covering 1.2M physicians and 50K+ facilities, natively built on Salesforce. Covers pre-launch through M&A commercial lifecycle.
**Pricing**: From $1,500/user.
**Strengths relative to AcuityMD**:
- Only medtech intelligence tool natively built within Salesforce CRM — strong appeal for SFDC-heavy organizations
- Covers full commercial lifecycle including pre-launch market sizing and M&A diligence
- Published pricing ($1,500/user) creates cost transparency that AcuityMD's hidden pricing lacks
**Weaknesses relative to AcuityMD**:
- Salesforce dependency limits appeal to non-SFDC organizations
- Smaller company (S2N Health is a consulting firm that built a product) — less product-first DNA
- Less modern standalone UX — relies on Salesforce interface rather than purpose-built UI

## Messaging Gap Analysis

Themes competitors emphasize that AcuityMD does not:
- **Data breadth and scale**: Definitive Healthcare and IQVIA both lead with massive, multi-domain datasets (payers, consumers, Rx, hospitals). AcuityMD focuses on medtech-relevant data but doesn't claim the broadest dataset.
- **Full product lifecycle**: Veeva and IQVIA position as end-to-end platforms covering clinical, regulatory, quality, AND commercial. AcuityMD is commercial-only — competitors can frame this as a point solution.
- **Affordability for small teams**: Alpha Sophia explicitly positions as the cheaper, faster alternative to AcuityMD. AcuityMD has no visible pricing, which lets competitors control the cost narrative.

Themes AcuityMD owns that competitors underplay:
- **Purpose-built for medtech sales teams**: "Founded by former MedTech professionals" and "a format reps actually use." No competitor matches this specificity — Veeva and IQVIA are pharma-first, Definitive is horizontal, Alpha Sophia is data-first.
- **Speed to value**: "3x faster sales process" and "2 weeks from outreach to close." Competitors talk about data comprehensiveness, not sales velocity.

## Positioning Opportunities

1. **"Built by medtech, for medtech" vs. pharma hand-me-downs** — Veeva and IQVIA built their platforms for pharma and adapted them for devices. AcuityMD could sharpen this wedge: medtech sales is procedure-driven and physician-relationship-dependent in ways that pharma CRMs fundamentally misunderstand.
2. **AI-native intelligence vs. raw data exports** — Definitive Healthcare and Alpha Sophia give you data to explore. AcuityMD could own the "insights, not just information" lane more aggressively — telling reps what to do next, not just showing them a dashboard.
3. **Mid-market sweet spot** — Veeva and IQVIA are too heavy for startups and growth-stage companies. Alpha Sophia is too light for scaling teams. AcuityMD sits in the middle but doesn't explicitly claim this lane. A clear "you've outgrown spreadsheets but you're not ready for Veeva" message could accelerate mid-market wins.
